History

The Start of it All

Making its debut in 1969 Nissan had produced the first high performance version of the GT-R. With a DOHC 2.0 liter inline six making 160hp. The GT-R was originally a four door sedan, but in 1971 they introduced a two-door coupe. The GT-R was stripped of all unnecessary weight to improve track performance

The 1980's

Nissan in the eighties introduced the iconic R31,R32, and 33 GT-R. These models began production in 1985. The kept its general body style to 2002. The R31 had the reintroduction of the DOHC inline six, capable of 210hp. In 1989 the R32 had a twin turbo six with AWD, pushing 280hp

The Movie Screen

Between 1999 and 2002 Nissan produced the GT-R R34. This GT-R became the priciest one to date coming in around $85,000. The GT-R was installed with a twin-turbo inline six pushing 276hp. The GT-R later hit the big screen in The Fast & Furious movies which created a large popularity for the car, which was driven by Paul Walker in the 2009 Fast and Furious.
